Natanam was founded with a vision to serve people and educate them in all art forms. The Institute of Dance was founded and is led by Dr Smt Raksha Karthik. Our vision is to introduce the future generation to the ancient and beautiful dance form of Bharatanatyam, music and mythology.

The Institute is committed to the time-tested ancient tradition of teaching, practice and performance, simultaneously exploring interpretations of the dance form. Natanam Foundation for Arts has been acclaimed for its efforts and has been appreciated highly by many people and organisations, and has also been invited to perform at many events. Students from the Institute have garnered awards and appreciations at various competitions.

Natanam has also been accredited by the International Dance Council — CID, the official umbrella organisation for all forms of dance in all countries, and a part of UNESCO.

An Arangetram — from the Tamil words arangam (stage) and etram (ascending) — is the formal debut recital of a Bharatanatyam dancer. It is the moment a student ascends the stage for the very first time as a solo artist, presenting a complete Margam before an audience, after years of rigorous training under a Guru.

More than a performance, the Arangetram is a rite of passage — a sacred ceremony that marks the transition from student to artist. It is an act of deep gratitude: to the Guru who shaped every mudra and footstep, to the parents who supported the journey, to the art form passed down through generations, and to the divine who animates it all.

The tradition is ancient, rooted in the temple-dance lineage where a dancer's debut was offered as a gift to the presiding deity. Today it continues as one of the most significant milestones in the life of a classical dancer — a lifelong memory of the first time the stage became home.

Saanvi has trained for over eight years under the guidance of Dr. Raksha Kartik, and today she offers this recital with devotion, humility, and joy.

Twelve Years in the Making

The Journey

Saanvi's love for dance began long before she started formal training. From the age of one, she would dance whenever she heard music. At three, she began learning ballet and later explored Bollywood, folk, western, and other dance forms, eagerly embracing every opportunity to perform and learn.

In 2014, when she was five years old, a chance encounter introduced her to the world of Bharatanatyam. While attending a theatre class, she noticed a Bharatanatyam class taking place in the next room. The grace, rhythm, and energy of the dancers immediately captured her imagination. From that moment, Bharatanatyam became an important part of her life.

As her passion for the art form grew, Saanvi continued her training with dedication and enthusiasm. In fifth grade, she came under the guidance of Acharya Dr. Raksha Karthik, with whom she has been training ever since. Under her guru's mentorship, Bharatanatyam became more than just a dance form — it became a source of discipline, creativity, confidence, and self-expression.

One of Saanvi's most memorable experiences was her first Bharatanatyam examination in sixth grade. Despite having a fractured arm at the time, and with the encouragement of Acharya Dr. Raksha Karthik, she went on to secure a distinction — a milestone that strengthened her resolve. From seventh grade onwards, dance opened doors to new experiences, new compositions, and lasting friendships with fellow dancers.

Today, Saanvi is an enthusiastic Grade 11 student at National Public School. She completed her Gejje Pooje on 29th April 2023 and has successfully completed the advanced-level Visharada Prathama examination. Beyond dance, she pursues Carnatic music, art, calisthenics, running, and trekking.

She remains deeply grateful for the guidance of her gurus — beginning with Guru Smitha Hegde, who first introduced her to Bharatanatyam, and continuing with Acharya Dr. Raksha Karthik — and for the unwavering support of her family and all who have walked this path with her.
